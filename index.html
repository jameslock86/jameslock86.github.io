Tontines Games

A tontine (ton-teen) is an investment fund created by a Neapolitan banker named Lorenzo de Tonti in 1653. It is a powerful retirement scheme that works like a group annuity. Each participant pays an agreed sum into the tontine, and receives a payout upon the maturity date. The death of a member of the tontine prior to the maturity date means the reallocation of their share to the surviving members, thus increasing the value of each share. Upon the death of the second to last member or reaching the maturity date, the tontine dissolves and entitles the remaining member or members all of the share distributions.
